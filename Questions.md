# Business Scenario

You have just joined an e-commerce company as a **Junior Data Analyst**.

The Sales Director has noticed that although the company appears to be generating healthy sales, profitability has not improved as expected.

You have been asked to analyze the order data and prepare insights for management.

You are not told where the problems are—you need to discover them.

---

# Your Tasks

## Part 1 – Data Understanding

- Explore the dataset.
- Identify the data types.
- Produce summary statistics.
- Explain what each column represents.

---

## Part 2 – Data Quality Assessment

Identify and document:

- Missing values
- Duplicate records
- Incorrect data types
- Impossible values
- Outliers
- Inconsistent formatting
- Any other data quality issues you notice

Then clean the dataset appropriately.

---

## Part 3 – Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Answer questions such as:

- Which region generated the highest sales?
- Which region generated the highest profit?
- Which product category sells the most?
- Which products generate the highest revenue?
- What is the average shipping time?
- Which payment method is used most often?
- Which age group spends the most?
- Which products are returned most frequently?
- Does a higher discount appear to increase sales?
- Are there any seasonal sales trends?

---

## Part 4 – Visualizations

Create appropriate charts, for example:

- Bar charts
- Line charts
- Histograms
- Boxplots
- Scatter plots
- Correlation heatmap

Choose chart types that best communicate your findings.

---

## Part 5 – Business Insights

Prepare a short report answering:

- What are the biggest issues in the data? -  negative sales value, 
- Which products perform best?
- Which products perform worst?
- Which regions need improvement?
- What recommendations would you make to management?
- If you could collect one additional column in the future, what would it be and why?
